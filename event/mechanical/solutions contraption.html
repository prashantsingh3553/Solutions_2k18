Event Description

No of team members: maximum 8

A Contraption is a chain of different steps, in which one steps triggers the next one leading to completion of final task. This event requires simple class room ideas along with innovation that can make the solution as complicated and fascinating as possible. Contraption is all about making most complicated solution to a simple problem.

This contraption is designed to pull students away from conventional problem-solving and push them into the endless chaos of imagination and intuitive thought. Instead of just "solving" the problem, teams have to make the solution as complicated and as convoluted as possible.

The Contraption should include 2 segments:-

●    Segment 1:

You are free to choose a minimum of 3 energy conversions (excluding the intermediate steps) and maximum is unbounded.

●    But we give you a list of intermediate steps (which are compulsory, of course).

●    Segment 2:

1. A vertical ring of inner diameter 30-40 cm will be provided. The ring will be placed at a distance of 2m from your setup. And at a height of 1m from the ground.

2. A ball of minimum 2cm will be provided, which has to be thrown into the ring using your setup from the first segment.

3. As the ball is successfully thrown into the ring, the contraption is completed!

Intermediate Steps (Any 4 of the given 5 is compulsory):

1. Host a flag with min cross sectional area of 10 cm x 10 cm and min height of 80cm and it should display Team name. (max points)

2. Open the book and it should display “SOLUTIONS’16”.

2. Light a candle.

3. Glow a bulb

4. Move a car (remote car! & better points)

Prizes worth INR 10000

Rules:

It will be a inter college event.

Maximum of 7 members in a team are allowed.

Each step contains fixed number of points, hence more the number of steps, greater is the chance of winning.

The maximum space given to the teams to complete their contraption is 6 feet x 6 feet.

Participants would be allowed to initiate only the first step and this energy conversion will NOT be counted.

At maximum, teams would be given only one extra chance to start the contraption all over again.

No interventions are allowed during execution

All the participating teams must submit an abstract (written or typed), one day before the event solutionsait04@gmail.com regarding the steps involved in the working of their contraption along with a simple flow chart. All the steps in the abstract must be executed.

NO alteration of any kind is allowed at any point of time in the event (that is your practical contraption should have the same steps as submitted in abstract). Otherwise 10 negative points for each alteration will be awarded.

No explosive materials should be used.

Judges‘decision shall be final and binding to all.

In case of a tie the team with lesser number of repetitive conversions will be given priority.

BASIC MATERIALS PROVIDED

• 220 Volts A.C. Supply

POINTS ALLOTMENT:

• All teams completing their task will be given 100 points.

• Bonus points (50) would be awarded to teams which complete the task in first chance.

• Teams can use parallel paths and would be awarded 50 points for each set of parallel paths.

• Marks evaluation of different energy conversions are:

 Mechanical energy-10 pts

 Electrical energy - 10 pts

 Chemical energy-15 pts

 Sound energy -15 pts

 Light energy – 15 pts

 Magnetic energy- 20 pts

NOTE: More than 3 repetitions of a particular energy conversion will not be awarded any points and third time half points will be awarded for that particular energy conversion.

Ex-(1)Mechanical to chemical energy pair is awarded only 3 times, for next time no points will be awarded.

Judging Criteria

1. Number of steps in the contraption

2. Number of energy conversions

3. Types of energy conversions.

4. Implementation of intermediate steps

5. Originality of the ideas and innovativeness

6. Robustness of the contraption

7. Number of trials and forced human interventions used(will lead to deduction of points)

8. Complexity of the overall contraption.

Abstract must have following steps:

• Blue print of contraption (with flow diagram/chart).

• Number of steps in the contraption (with explanation of each step).

Number of energy conversions.
